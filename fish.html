DRINK LIKE A FISH

MEANING: DRINK EXCESSIVE AMOUNTS OF ALCOHOL.

“Give me the bottle, I can drink like a Fish now, like an Elephant.”

A clear allusion to a fish’s open-mouthed taking in of water, this saying first appeared in Fletcher and Shirley’s work The Night-Walker, Or The Little Thief in 1640. And while ‘drink like a fish’ caught on, ‘drink like an elephant sadly did not.
